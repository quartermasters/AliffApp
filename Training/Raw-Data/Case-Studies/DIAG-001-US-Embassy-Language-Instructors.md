# Diagnosis Case Study: US Embassy Language Instructors

**ID:** DIAG-001
**Date:** 2025-11-11
**Service Category:** GOVCON
**Solicitation Type:** Re-compete

**Aliff Roles with Access:** [OPS, TRAINER, CEO]
**Sensitivity Level:** proprietary

---

## What the RFP Said (The Symptoms)

**Stated Requirements:**
- Provide qualified English and Urdu language instructors
- English instruction for local Pakistani staff
- Urdu instruction for US diplomatic staff
- Specific education requirements
- Experience criteria
- Security clearance requirements
- Standard procedures and compliance

**Evaluation Criteria:**
Technical approach, qualifications of instructors, past performance, price

**Timeline:**
Standard transition period specified in RFP

---

## Diagnosis Process (Finding the Root Cause)

**Questions We Asked:**
1. Why is this contract being re-competed?
2. Who are the current instructors and how are they performing?
3. What are the operational constraints for transitioning to new contractor?
4. What timeline is required for security clearances for new hires?
5. What is the agency's real pain point beyond the stated requirements?

**Research We Conducted:**
- Reviewed incumbent contractor performance
- Researched US Embassy security clearance timelines in Pakistan
- Analyzed transition requirements

**Stakeholder Engagement:**
Engaged with contracting officer to understand operational constraints and transition concerns.

---

## What We Discovered (The Real Pain Points)

**Real Pain Points:**
- Agency wants to keep current instructors but change contractor
- Current instructors are already security cleared and performing well
- Embassy cannot afford operational gap in language instruction
- Mission-critical need for continuity

**Hidden Constraints:**
- **7-8 month security clearance timeline** for new hires in Pakistan
- Operational disruption unacceptable for diplomatic mission
- Current instructors willing to transition to new contractor
- Political/contractual reasons prevent simply extending current contract

**Competitive Insights:**
Any competitor proposing to hire new instructors would create 7-8 month gap in service delivery - automatic disqualification in practice.

---

## Win Strategy (The Prescription)

**Win Themes:**
1. **Zero Operational Disruption:** Hire existing security-cleared instructors, service continues seamlessly
2. **Mission Continuity:** No gap in language instruction for diplomatic mission
3. **Proven Performance:** Retain instructors with demonstrated track record
4. **Risk Mitigation:** Eliminate 7-8 month security clearance risk

**Solution Approach:**
- Transition plan explicitly included hiring current instructors
- Emphasized continuity of proven performers
- Highlighted elimination of security clearance delay risk
- Addressed agency's unstated need (keep instructors, change contractor)

**Differentiators:**
- Only proposal that solved the real problem (continuity)
- Competitors focused on hiring "qualified" new instructors (7-8 month gap)
- We addressed what RFP couldn't explicitly state

---

## Outcome

**Result:** won

**Feedback Received:**
Agency confirmed continuity of service was critical evaluation factor. Our understanding of operational constraints and security clearance timelines demonstrated superior grasp of their needs.

**Root Cause Analysis:**
Won because we diagnosed the unstated requirement. Competitors responded to what was written; we solved what was needed.

---

## Lessons Learned

**Lesson 1:**
- **What we learned:** Solicitations often cannot explicitly state real requirements due to procurement rules or political constraints
- **How to apply:** Always ask "Why NOW? Why re-compete?" to discover unstated drivers

**Lesson 2:**
- **What we learned:** Operational constraints (security clearances, transition timelines) often matter more than stated technical requirements
- **How to apply:** Investigate operational realities that affect contract execution, not just stated deliverables

**Lesson 3:**
- **What we learned:** "Fever is just a symptom" - RFP describes what they want, diagnosis reveals what they need
- **How to apply:** Treat every RFP as symptoms requiring diagnosis before prescription

---

## Patterns Identified

**Reusable Patterns:**
- **Security Clearance Timeline Pattern:** When security clearances required + short transition timeline = incumbent/cleared personnel strongly preferred
- **Re-compete with Continuity Need:** Re-compete RFPs often hide "keep performers, change contractor" requirement
- **Operational Constraint Trumps Technical:** Operational constraints (clearances, transitions) often outweigh technical qualifications in evaluation
